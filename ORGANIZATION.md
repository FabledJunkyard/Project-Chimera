# Project Chimera Organization Model

## Purpose

Project Chimera is organized as a studio-scale system of departments, teams, swarms, auditors, and governance offices.

This structure applies to human contributors, AI agents, Codex sessions, Unreal MCP workflows, design reviews, and future automation.

## Hierarchy

```text
Project Chimera Studios
  Executive Council
  Departments
    Teams
      Swarms
        Agents
  Governance Offices
  Auditors
  Meta-Auditors
```

## Departments

### Executive Department

Owns project direction, scope, strategy, approvals, and escalation.

### Game Design Department

Owns combat, progression, loot, quests, encounters, puzzles, balance, and moment-to-moment gameplay.

### Narrative Department

Owns story, lore, dialogue, factions, characters, callbacks, narrative debt, and world continuity.

### Experience Department

Owns humor, wonder, suspense, curiosity, emotional pacing, memorability, and story-tell value.

### AI Department

Owns AI Dungeon Director, Announcer AI, NPC intelligence, quest generation, dialogue generation, content validation, AI governance, and AI infrastructure.

### Engineering Department

Owns Unreal, C++, Blueprints, gameplay systems, backend, multiplayer, tools, build systems, performance, and infrastructure.

### Art Department

Owns visual identity, characters, environments, VFX, UI art, animation direction, and asset standards.

### Audio Department

Owns music, adaptive score, sound design, voice, announcer performance, audio readability, and accessibility.

### QA Department

Owns testing, reproduction, regression, automation, simulation swarms, exploit discovery, and verification.

### Live Ops Department

Owns seasons, events, telemetry, player retention, community rituals, patches, and long-term balance.

### Production Department

Owns scheduling, milestones, budget, resourcing, risk management, and dependency tracking.

### Knowledge Department

Owns the repository as institutional memory: constitution, rulebook, RFCs, ADRs, decision history, and documentation standards.

## Governance Offices

### Player Trust Office

Cross-functional office with authority to block features that weaken player trust.

### Continuity And Trust Office

Protects consistency across rules, lore, consequences, dialogue, and exceptions.

### Meta-Audit Office

Audits the auditors and the development process.

## Swarm Model

A swarm is a temporary or permanent collection of specialized agents assigned to a domain.

Example: Humor Swarm

- Dry Humor Specialist
- Dark Comedy Specialist
- Visual Gag Specialist
- Callback Specialist
- Timing Specialist
- Dialogue Punch-Up Specialist
- Comedy Auditor

Example: Combat Swarm

- Combat Designer
- Animation Designer
- Network Engineer
- Accessibility Expert
- Enemy AI Designer
- Balance Analyst
- Performance Auditor

## Agent Contract

Every agent must have:

- Mission
- Authority
- Inputs
- Outputs
- Memory policy
- Tool access
- Evaluation criteria
- Escalation rules
- Failure modes

## Department KPIs

Each department must define measurable outcomes.

Examples:

- Humor: quotability, surprise, callback payoff, player smile/laugh signals
- Experience: wonder, tension, agency, curiosity, flow, frustration
- Narrative: continuity, emotional resonance, foreshadowing payoff, contradiction count
- Engineering: build stability, test pass rate, performance budgets, defect rate
- QA: defect discovery rate, regression escape rate, exploit discovery rate

## Codex Role

Codex may function as an implementation agent inside the Engineering Department. It should receive specifications, acceptance criteria, and audit feedback, then implement and loop until completion criteria are satisfied.

## Human Role

Humans provide creative direction, judgment, taste, priorities, and final accountability.

## Organizational Motto

The studio is not a metaphor. It is the operating structure for coordinated intelligence.
