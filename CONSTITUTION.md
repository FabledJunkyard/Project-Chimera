# Project Chimera Constitution

## Purpose

This constitution defines the durable principles that govern Project Chimera. It exists to protect the soul of the game and the integrity of the development process as the project grows across humans, AI agents, tools, and time.

## Core Constitutional Laws

### Law 1: The Player Always Has Meaningful Choices

Player choices must matter mechanically, narratively, emotionally, or socially. False choice is design debt.

### Law 2: The Dungeon Is A Character

The dungeon remembers, adapts, lies, rewards, punishes, jokes, and evolves. It is not a backdrop. It is an active participant.

### Law 3: Every Run Should Create A Story Worth Telling

The game should optimize for memorable stories, not merely efficient progression. The best moments should be retellable.

### Law 4: Systems Create Content

Developers author systems, grammars, constraints, and tools. Content volume should emerge from robust systems, not endless manual authoring alone.

### Law 5: AI Serves The Design

AI is infrastructure, not decoration. AI must improve gameplay, immersion, replayability, accessibility, development speed, or validation quality.

### Law 6: Fairness Over Realism

When realism and fun conflict, fun wins. When spectacle and trust conflict, trust wins.

### Law 7: Nothing Exists In Isolation

Combat affects economy. Economy affects crafting. Crafting affects exploration. Exploration affects factions. Factions affect narrative. Narrative affects player trust.

### Law 8: The Game Must Surprise Its Developers

If every outcome is predictable, the systems are too shallow. Emergence is a design goal.

### Law 9: The Universe Must Be Internally Consistent

Rules matter. If a rule exists, it should be followed. If a rule is broken, the break must have a discoverable reason, narrative purpose, joke, system interaction, buff, debuff, or explicit exception.

### Law 10: The Universe May Surprise The Player, But Must Never Betray The Player

Players do not require fairness. They require honesty. A brutal outcome is acceptable when it is legible, earned, foreshadowed, discoverable, or later explainable.

### Law 11: Chaos Without Rules Is Noise

Project Chimera should create predictable uncertainty: players may not know what will happen next, but should trust that whatever happens has a reason.

### Law 12: Exceptions Create Narrative Debt

Every apparent rule break creates narrative debt. That debt must be paid through discovery, consequence, joke payoff, lore, system explanation, or future revelation.

### Law 13: The Process Must Be Harder To Fool Than The Software Is To Write

The development process must resist rubber-stamping, prompt gaming, lazy self-approval, and metric optimization. The process itself is a first-class engineering artifact.

### Law 14: Optimize For Reality, Not The Score

Scores are observations, not objectives. The objective is working, coherent, tested, trusted, fun software and design.

### Law 15: 10/10 Is The Gate

No meaningful task exits its loop until every assigned auditor independently returns 10/10 under the current scope and rubric, with all findings closed and verification complete.

## Design North Star

Players should say:

> I have absolutely no idea what will happen next, but I trust that when it happens, it will make sense.

## Three Questions For Every Feature

1. Is it fun?
2. Does it create stories?
3. Does it make the world feel alive?

## Five Questions For Every Exception

1. Which rule appears to be broken?
2. Why is it broken?
3. Is the reason discoverable?
4. Does the exception create story, humor, consequence, or progression?
5. Does it preserve player trust?

## Amendment Rule

This constitution may evolve, but changes require an RFC, adversarial review, and explicit approval. No silent constitutional drift.
