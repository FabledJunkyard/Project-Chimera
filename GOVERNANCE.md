# Project Chimera Governance

## Purpose

Governance defines how Project Chimera makes, records, challenges, audits, and revises decisions.

The goal is not bureaucracy. The goal is to prevent invisible drift, hidden debt, contradictory design, and AI-generated self-approval.

## Decision Types

### Constitutional Decisions

Changes to the durable laws of the project. Require RFC, adversarial review, and explicit approval.

### Architectural Decisions

Long-lived technical decisions. Recorded as ADRs.

### Design Decisions

Gameplay, narrative, economy, combat, quest, humor, and experience decisions. Recorded as design specs or RFCs.

### Implementation Decisions

Code-level and asset-level decisions. Recorded in commits, pull requests, implementation notes, and tests.

## Decision Workflow

1. Proposal drafted.
2. Relevant swarm reviews independently.
3. Auditors evaluate greenfield.
4. Findings become work items.
5. Proposal revised.
6. Fresh audit runs.
7. Loop continues until 10/10.
8. Executive or domain owner approves.
9. Decision is recorded.

## Required Review Modes

Every major decision should receive at least one review from each relevant mode:

- Creative review
- Systems review
- Technical review
- Player trust review
- Production risk review
- Audit review

## Governance Offices

### Executive Council

Owns strategic alignment, scope discipline, and final escalation decisions.

### Player Trust Office

Protects the relationship between player and game. Reviews fairness, consistency, monetization, surprise, rule breaks, and perceived betrayal.

### Continuity Office

Protects lore, rules, narrative debt, callbacks, and consequence tracking.

### Meta-Audit Office

Audits the development system itself. Looks for prompt gaming, approval bias, stale rubrics, auditor weakness, and systemic blind spots.

## RFCs

RFCs are used for proposals requiring discussion or tradeoff analysis.

RFCs must include:

- Context
- Problem
- Proposal
- Alternatives
- Risks
- Swarm review summary
- Audit findings
- Scoring or finding summary
- Decision
- Follow-up work

## ADRs

ADRs are used for long-lived technical decisions.

ADRs must include:

- Status
- Context
- Decision
- Consequences
- Alternatives rejected
- Audit requirements

## Anti-Drift Rule

No major principle, rule, or architecture may be silently weakened. Any relaxation of standards must be explicit, reviewed, and recorded.

## Rejection Is Useful

Rejected ideas should be recorded when they contain valuable reasoning. A rejected idea can prevent future rediscovery of the same bad path.

## Governance Motto

Move deliberately enough that future systems can trust past decisions.
